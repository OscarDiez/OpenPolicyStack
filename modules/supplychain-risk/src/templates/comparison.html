{% if sector %}{{ sector }} — {% endif %} {{ segment }} — Geopolitical Perspective Comparison

EU, US, and China risk scores for each component, derived from perspective-specific adversary configurations (methodology section 5.3). Divergence measures the spread between the highest and lowest perspective score for the same component.

How scores differ by perspective: The geopolitical pillar score is recalculated per perspective using distinct adversary sets. EU treats Russia, China, Iran, Belarus, and North Korea as adversaries. US treats Russia, China, Iran, North Korea, and Cuba as adversaries (Venezuela removed, 2026). China treats the United States, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, India, Australia, and the UK as adversaries. All other pillars (HHI, shelf-life, substitutability, lead-time, regulatory, strategic impact) are perspective-neutral.
